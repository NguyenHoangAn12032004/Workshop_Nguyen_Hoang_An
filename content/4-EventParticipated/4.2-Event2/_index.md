# First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ) Meetup 13-06-2026

### Event Details
| | |
|---|---|
| **Event Name** | First Cloud AI Journey — Monthly Meetup |
| **Date** | 13/06/2026 |
| **Location** | AWS Vietnam Office / Google Meet (Hybrid) |
| **Organizer** | AWS Study Group Vietnam |
| **Role** | Listener & Learner |

### Event Description
This monthly meetup brought together interns, mentors, and industry experts from the AWS community to share practical career experiences, cloud engineering practices, and corporate culture insights.

### Main Content
The meetup featured three main sharing sessions:

1. **What Does a DevOps Engineer Really Do?** (*Speaker: Trong H. Truong - DevOps Engineer @ Endava Vietnam*)
   - Demystified the common perception of DevOps (not just writing CI/CD or fixing production at midnight) and focused on real-life roles.
   - Emphasized learning fundamentals first: Linux, networking basics, programming languages (Python/Golang), Git, CI/CD, and containers.
   - Key warning: "Copying commands is not the same as understanding." A good engineer learns to ask "why" before "how", thinks in systems, and uses AI to leverage skills rather than switching off their brain.

2. **From First Cloud AI Journey to AWS Partner** (*Speaker: Danh Hoàng Hiếu Nghị - AI Engineer & AWS Community Builder*)
   - Shared his personal growth journey starting as an FCAJ participant to becoming an AWS Partner.
   - Introduced community growth pathways like the AWS Student Builder Group and AWS Community Builder Program.
   - Encouraged students that getting a job is just the beginning: "Write your own history!"

3. **Real-world Stories to MNC Corporate Culture** (*Speakers: Cường Nguyễn & Đạt Phạm - AWS / Data Analytics Engineers*)
   - Walked through the day-to-day responsibilities of a Data Analytics Engineer and the critical need for critical thinking, communication, and storytelling with data.
   - Outlined standard recruitment pipelines in MNCs (ATS screening, English HR interview, technical assessment, STAR model interviews, and leadership fit).
   - Introduced core enterprise cultures:
     - **No-Blame Post-Mortem:** Focus on fixing systems and identifying root causes rather than blaming individuals when failures occur.
     - **Caring & Inclusive:** Putting people at the center of development.
     - **"Đúng Việc" Philosophy (TS. Giản Tư Trung):** The core principles of being a Human, a Professional, and a Citizen.

### Lessons Learned & Personal Contribution
As a beginner exploring cloud engineering for the first time, this event was an invaluable source of motivation and experience:
- **Focus on Fundamentals:** I realized that tools will always change, but fundamentals (Linux, networking, and programming) remain. I must avoid simply copying commands and focus on understanding the underlying mechanisms of cloud platforms.
- **Career Motivation:** Seeing the speaker's journey from an FCAJ intern to an AWS Partner showed me what is possible with continuous learning and active participation in the builder community.
- **Engineering Mindset:** The "No-Blame Post-Mortem" culture taught me a professional way to look at mistakes — as system problems to solve rather than personal failures. This is a very comforting and constructive mindset for a beginner.
- **"Đúng Việc" Compass:** Doing my job right and acting professionally from day one will guide my growth throughout this learning journey.
