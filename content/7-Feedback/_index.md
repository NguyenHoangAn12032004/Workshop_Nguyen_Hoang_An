## Feedback on the FCAJ Internship Program

### What I Liked
1. **Structured Learning Path**: The 4-week AWS foundation phase gave me solid knowledge before jumping into project work. The progression from individual services (EC2, S3, Lambda) to integrated architectures was well-designed.

2. **Real Project Experience**: Building LingoRise from scratch — a production-grade IELTS/TOEIC platform — gave me practical experience that theoretical courses cannot provide. Working with 8+ AWS services in a single project was invaluable.

3. **English Documentation Requirement**: Being required to write all technical documentation in English significantly improved my technical communication skills. Writing README files, API specs, and blog posts in English is now a natural part of my workflow.

4. **Mentor Support**: Having access to experienced mentors (Nguyễn Gia Hưng – hunggia@amazon.com) who could provide guidance on AWS best practices and architectural decisions was extremely helpful.

5. **AWS Study Group Community**: Being part of a larger community of AWS learners provided motivation and opportunities to learn from peers' projects and experiences.

### Suggestions for Improvement
1. **More Hands-on Workshops**: While the self-study materials were good, more interactive workshops demonstrating complex AWS patterns (multi-account strategies, advanced VPC networking) would deepen understanding.

2. **Code Review Sessions**: Regular code review sessions between interns and mentors would help catch bad practices early and expose everyone to different coding approaches.

3. **Peer Demo Sessions**: Hosting informal demo sessions between intern teams before the formal Demo Day would help practice presentation skills in a low-pressure environment.

4. **Mobile Development Guidance**: Since many real-world applications need mobile support, including a brief module on React Native or PWA deployment on AWS would be valuable.

## Sharing with Future Interns

### Tips for Success in the FCAJ Bootcamp

**Week 1–4 (Foundation Phase)**:
- Don't just read — do every hands-on lab. The muscle memory of typing AWS CLI commands and clicking through the Console is what sticks.
- Start your worklog from day 1. Don't wait until week 10 to backfill — you'll forget the details.
- Take screenshots of everything you build. They're invaluable for your blog posts and report.

**Week 5–9 (Project Phase)**:
- Choose a project you're genuinely interested in. You'll be spending 5+ weeks on it — passion makes the difference between a mediocre and exceptional result.
- Set up CI/CD early. Being able to deploy with `sam build && sam deploy` saves hours of manual work.
- Write your blog posts as you go. Trying to write 3 blog posts in week 12 is painful.

**Week 10–12 (Reporting Phase)**:
- Rehearse your Demo Day presentation at least twice. The `FINAL_DEMO_CHECKLIST.md` approach (17 ordered steps) saved me from freezing during the real presentation.
- Your internship report website is a permanent portfolio piece. Invest time in making it look professional.

### Tools I Found Most Useful
| Tool | Why |
|------|-----|
| **AWS SAM CLI** | Infrastructure-as-code made deployment repeatable and version-controlled |
| **React Query** | Made frontend data fetching and caching trivially easy |
